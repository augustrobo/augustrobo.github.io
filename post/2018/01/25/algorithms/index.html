Karatsuba Multiplication

  • Problem: multiplication of two $n$-digit numbers $x, y$
  • simple method: $O(n^2)$ operations
  • recursive method:

$$ x = 10^{n/2}a+b, y = 10^{n/2}c+d, \text{ where }a,b,c,d\text{ are }\frac{n}{2}-\text{digit numbers} $$

$$ \Rightarrow xy = 10^nac+ 10^{n/2}(ad+bc)+bd\ \ \ \ \ \ (*) $$

Algorithm #1 (Naive method)

  • recursively compute $ac$, $ad$, $bc$, and $bd$
  • then compute (*)

running time $$ T(n) \begin{cases} =O(1), &n=1(\text{base case})\newline \leq 4T(\frac{n}{2}) + O(n), &n\geq 1(\text{4 subproblems and linear time addition}) \end{cases} $$ By master method,

$$ a = 4 > 2^1 = b^d (\text{case 3}) \Rightarrow T(n) = O(n^{\log_ba})=O(n^2) $$ Algorithm #2 (Gauss method)

  • recursively compute $ac$, $bd$, $(a+b)(c+d)$
  • $ad+bc = (a+b)(c+d)-(ac+bd)$
  • then compute (*)

running time $$ T(n) \begin{cases} =O(1), &n=1(\text{base case})\newline \leq 3T(\frac{n}{2}) + O(n), &n\geq 1(\text{3 subproblems and linear time addition}) \end{cases} $$ By master method,

$$ a = 3 > 2^1 = b^d (\text{case 3}) \Rightarrow T(n) = O(n^{\log_ba})=O(n^{\log_23})=O(n^{1.59}) $$ Better than simple method!
